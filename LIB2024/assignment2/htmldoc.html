Steph's Book Reccs

My sister-in-law gifted me Casey McQuiston's book for Christmas stating, and I quote, "I've been holding onto it for 4 months knowing you'd love it because it's both gay and magical!"

Not needing any other reason, I dove into One Last Stop as soon as I left my parents to cat-sit for a friend of mine over New Year's Eve -- devouring it within the week.

Written in 2021, this lesbian romance novel tells the story of August Landry who moves to New York to finally finish university, get a job, and maybe fall in love. But just as she's starting to get her life together, August's life unravels through time and space.

Is August ready for the adventure the NYC subway is ready to throw her way?

What if that adventure was named Jane Su? A punk lesbian from the 1970s trapped in limbo, between time and space, on the Q for nearly 50 years?

Well, I suppose that's for you to find out with August since she's about to bump right into Jane on her first day at NYU.

The fact that the novel takes place in New York City, in my opinion, makes the perfect location for a story chocked-full of mysterious time-travelling and magical realism.
